Cube Root

Background

The calculation of the cube roots can be traced back to the Babylonian Empire. A method for extracting cube roots appears in The Nine Chapters on the Mathematical Art, a Chinese mathematical text compiled around the 2nd century BCE and commented on by Liu Hui. The cube root was then developed in 499 C.E by Indian mathematician Aryabhata when he found a method for finding the cube root of numbers with many digits.

The cube root can be used to find one side of a cube. Keplerâ€™s third law of planetary motion involves cube roots of physical quantities. Architects and astronomers also use the cube root function.

How to Graph

An easy way to graph a cube root function is just to plug points into x and plot the y-values. But there is an easy way to start graphing in the form y = ∛x - h + k. A pattern that appears in every cube root graph is that the center of your graph will always be the opposite of (h, k). Also when picking points to plot onto the graph make it easier on yourself and pick points of x that add/subtract with h into a perfect cube. Remember decimals are hard and bad to cube. The domain and range for a cube root function will always be (-∞, ∞), so there are a lot of numbers that can be chosen.
